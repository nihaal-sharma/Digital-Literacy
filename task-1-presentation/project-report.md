For this project, I went with Canva because its drag-and-drop setup and academic icons made it easy to turn a dry topic into something actually worth looking at. My design, "Digital Literacy: Your Key to the Future," is a one-page infographic designed specifically for my batchmates. It breaks down three big areas: what digital literacy really means in 2026, non-negotiable safety habits like 2FA, and how to build a professional "vibe" on LinkedIn.
I chose a clean, three-column layout with high-contrast colors so the main points pop even if someone is just scrolling past. What I found most interesting—and a bit eye-opening—was looking into "digital footprints." It’s wild how our smallest online moves can stick around and shape our future careers. The hardest part was definitely the "less is more" challenge; I had to strip away the technical jargon and condense big security concepts into punchy bullet points so the slide didn’t feel cluttered. In the end, I wanted to create a quick cheat sheet that helps us all navigate the web a bit smarter.
