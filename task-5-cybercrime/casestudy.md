Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data by pretending to be a trusted source.

A typical phishing attack happens in steps. First, the attacker sends a fake email or message that looks like it is from a bank or popular website. The message often creates urgency, such as warning about account suspension. Next, the user is asked to click a link that leads to a fake website. This website looks real, but it is controlled by the attacker. When the user enters their login details, the attacker collects the information.

Students, employees, and elderly people are common targets. Many users do not verify links or sender details.

The consequences can include financial loss, hacked accounts, and identity theft.

To stay safe, users should avoid clicking unknown links, verify emails, and check website URLs carefully
