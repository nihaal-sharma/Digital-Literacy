UPI PIN is for Paying, Not Receiving: You NEVER need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If a "buyer" asks for it, it’s a scam.

Verify UPI IDs: Before hitting 'Pay,' double-check the receiver's name that appears in the app. Scammers often use names like "Customer Support."

Use 2FA: Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all social media and email accounts to prevent identity theft.

Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking: Never log into your bank or UPI app while connected to free college cafe or station Wi-Fi; use your mobile data instead.

Check URLs Carefully: Before entering credentials, ensure the site is https:// and look for slight misspellings (e.g., vlt.edu instead of vit.edu).

Secure Your SIM: Set a SIM card PIN. If your phone is stolen, the thief cannot use your SIM in another device to generate OTPs.

Limit Social Media Exposure: Avoid posting photos of your ID cards, hall tickets, or boarding passes, which contain sensitive QR codes and PRNs.

App Permissions: Regularly audit your apps. A simple calculator or wallpaper app does not need access to your contacts or SMS.

If you are a victim of fraud, report it immediately:

Website: cybercrime.gov.in

Helpline: Call 1930 (National Cyber Crime Helpline)
