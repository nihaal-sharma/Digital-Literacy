While researching UPI scams, I was most surprised by how scammers use social engineering rather than technical hacking.
They rely on the victim's "transaction muscle memory"—the habit of quickly entering a PIN—to bypass high-end bank security.
It is alarming how a simple phone call can manipulate someone into handing over their savings.

As a result, I will personally change my habit of scanning QR codes blindly.
I will now treat every QR code as a potential "Pull" request and will never open my UPI app via a link sent by a stranger,
choosing instead to manually enter a known ID if a payment is necessary.
